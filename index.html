What Is Included in GDP?

Sort each card for the U.S. into the correct bucket. GDP includes the market value of final goods and services produced within the U.S. during the period. Many items are excluded to avoid double counting or because they are not production.

How this works
  • Included: new final goods/services produced domestically during the period (incl. investment & inventories, gov’t purchases, exports).
  • Intermediate goods are excluded to avoid double counting (value shows up in the final good).
  • Transfers & financial transactions are excluded (not payments for current production).
  • Used goods are excluded (not current production). Some services around used goods (e.g., dealer fees) are included.
